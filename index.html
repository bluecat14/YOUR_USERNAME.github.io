Recipe #1

  • Time: 25 minutes
  • Serves: 8
Ingredients Quantity
Flour 1 cup
Sugar 3 tbsp
Baking Powder 1 tsp
Baking Soda 1/2 tsp
Salt 1/4 tsp
Half and Half 3/4 cup + 1 tbsp
Apple Cider Vinegar 1 tbsp
Egg 1
Vegetable Oil 2 tbsp
Vanilla 1 tsp
Chocolate Chips 1/2 cup

Step 1: In a large mixing bowl, whisk together dry ingredients (flour, baking powder, baking soda and salt).

Step 2: In a measuring cup, combine half and half and apple cider vinegar. Stir few times and let sit 2 to 3 minutes. It should thicken up and half and half should sour.

Step 3: Add egg, oil and vanilla to medium bowl or large measuring cup. Whisk until smooth. Add half and half and vinegar mixture. Whisk to combine. Add the wet ingredients and chocolate chips to dry ingredients and stir to combine. Do not overmix. The batter should be slightly lumpy. After few minutes (while the griddle pan heats up), the batter will thicken and have bubbles

Step 4:Using an ice cream scoop, scoop the batter (1 scoop per pancake) and fry on one side until bubbles form holes around the edges and towards the center. Flip pancakes and fry on the other side for 10 to 15 seconds. I always melt 1 tablespoon of butter on the griddle while it heats up.

Step 5:Serve warm with a slice of butter and drizzle of maple syrup.

Recipe #3

  • Time: 2 hours 30 min
  • Serves: 24
Ingredients Quantity
Melted Butter 1/2 cup
Sugar 1/3 cup
Brown Sugar 1/2 cup
Egg 1
Vanilla 1 tsp
Baking Soda 1/2 tsp
Salt 1/2 tsp
Flour 1 1/2 cups
Chocolate Chips 1 1/2 cups

Step 1: Note: This dough requires chilling.

Step 2: Place melted butter in the bowl of a stand mixer fitted with the paddle attachment (or a large bowl if using a hand mixer). Add granulated and brown sugars and mix on low speed until the mixture is smooth. Mix in egg and vanilla extract and mix on medium speed until combined.

Step 3: Mix in baking soda and salt, then slowly mix in flour and mix just until the batter is smooth and comes together. Be sure to scrape the sides of the bowl during mixing. Slowly mix in chocolate chips.

Step 4: Line a cookie sheet with a silicone baking mat or parchment paper. Scoop 2 tablespoon balls of dough onto the cookie sheet. Spacing doesn’t matter because you will be chilling the dough. Cover with plastic wrap and chill for at least 2 hours.

Step 5: Preheat oven to 350°F. Line a second cookie sheet with parchment pap er or a silicone baking mat.

Step 6:Remove the chilled cookie dough balls from the refrigerator and space them 2-inches apart on the cookie sheets. Bake (2 tablesp oon sized cookies) for 11-15 minutes, or until the edges a re a light golden and the tops are no longer glossy. Let cool on the cookie sheets at least 10 minutes before removing.
